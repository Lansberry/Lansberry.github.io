# Film Pitches & Ideas

*Running list — concepts, premises, and half-formed ideas. Last updated: June 19, 2026.*

---

## Short Films

- **Park Avenue Pub Series** — All stories told from inside the pub. Short 10-min clips. Episodes: Luke driving to multiple McDonald's for a full toy collection but keeps getting the same one. Playing soccer while we look 23 and everyone else is 12. Sailing down the St. Lawrence River. Bill Long being our neighbor. AWess and his tapas. Braden dumping water on Nick's head while he's drunk. Nate (sober) not remembering the fish tank bar.

- **Escape Room Commercial** — 10-second clips of different escape rooms. Quick-cut promo style.

- **Cult Tattoo** — Person has an ancestry that leaves a tattoo on their body that indicates they're dying. Like a lotus tree where the leaves fall off. Mystery/horror tone.

- **Credits In Your Face** — Short film where the credits are displayed in-your-face throughout the entire film, not just at the end.

- **Escape Room Friends** — Short film: friends in an escape room. One of them has completely off-the-wall theories that don't make sense but somehow solves every puzzle.

- **Job Interview / Heaven** — Short film: a guy sits down for a job interview. The interviewer turns out to be the entrance interview into heaven.

- **iFilm** — A movie shot completely on an iPhone. That's the whole bit. Make it look incredible anyway.

- **Tell-Tale Smoke Alarm** — Short adaptation of The Tell-Tale Heart, but it's a man who can't figure out how to take the battery out of a dying smoke alarm. Hides it out of embarrassment. Slowly driven crazy hearing it while nobody else can.

- **Deaf Dad / Dementia** — Short: a deaf old man's home is being invaded. Reveal: the "intruder" is his son. The old man has dementia.

- **The Grieving Friend** — A guy passes away. One of his friends cannot come to terms with it and keeps imagining him in daily life as if nothing happened. Big reveal at the end.

- **Tinder Date Triangle** — A guy is invited to a show as part of a Tinder date. His "match" also invited someone else. The two randoms hit it off and leave the match in the dust.

- **Vampire Lore Correction** — A vampire explains, calmly and with mounting frustration, how humans got literally everything wrong about vampire lore. Comedy.

- **Smoke Alarm Tell-Tale** *(see above — same pitch, different framing angle)*

- **Churchesque Amnesia** — A church sign reads: "The best way to heal a hurt is amnesia." The next stretch of road is an eerily shaped hill with perfect grass, one round tree, one brown horse. Everyone in the car gets amnesia.

## Feature Films

- **SuperZero** — A series (or film) about an ordinary guy with an ordinary day job — just very annoyed that everyone around him is becoming superpowered and he's still working at Best Buy. The whole point is he's completely normal and increasingly inconvenienced.

- **Love or Rust** — Based on Rusty from BattleBots. A guy experiences heartbreak, falls in love with BattleBots, builds Rusty, then falls in love with another bot builder who gives him an ultimatum: her team or Rusty. He picks Rusty. Breakup battle happens. She wins. He's sad. Cute audience member offers to help fix Rusty.

- **BattleBots Cinematic Universe** — Starting with two: *Gone-dala* (Stabby boat). *Myrrhiah Carry* — a Christmas bot covered in lights playing "All I Want For Christmas Is You" while secretly firing EMPs masked by the music. She's the villain.

- **Bit Boyz** — Office-style mockumentary following 3 guys who manage Bitcoin mining operations out of a shipping container. (Poutrain)

- **Reboot: Smokey and the Bandit** — Same energy, updated with a Bitcoin storyline. Cross-country run, but the cargo is crypto.

- **Film Race Reality TV** — 4 teams have a limited time to write, shoot, and cut a pilot. The show follows the creative process. Pilots launch on streaming and real-time viewer voting picks a winner. Winner gets a guaranteed season.

- **A Man Magnet Fishing** — Recently single guy picks up magnet fishing as a hobby. Starts pulling bodies out of the water — serial killer's work. Reluctant investigation follows.

- **Antiquers as Serial Killers** — Antiquers kill families and sell their belongings to fund something — maybe buying an organ on the black market for a dying wife. Thriller.

- **Dead Internet Society** — Loosely based on the Dead Internet Theory. A pseudo-dystopian world where the internet has become so oversaturated with hallucinating AI that it's useless. Society has reverted to pre-internet technology. Paperback books, physical directories, no social media. Someone starts to notice cracks in the "clean" world.

- **Of Life and Debt** — A person is given the option to pay off their debt with their remaining time alive.

- **Halloween Soul Park** — A Halloween seasonal park appears. Everything seems free. The customers are unknowingly paying with pieces of their souls.

- **D&Delusion** — A DM has a mental episode mid-game and can no longer discern what's real from what's in the campaign. Slow burn psychological horror.

- **The AI Actor** — An actor gets so lazy he creates an AI avatar and lets it do his movies for him. The avatar takes on a life of its own. He discovers it while watching TV — a film he has no memory of making.

- **Aunt Mom** — A woman leaves a stagnant career to take care of her sister's daughter after her sister dies of cancer. Struggles of pseudo-motherhood and self-realization.

- **Private Affairs** — The opposite of public relations: a firm whose entire job is to actively suppress information and counter unwanted attention. What do they actually know, and for whom?

- **Rhythm Game God** — A woman has become the greatest player of a particular undisclosed rhythm game. The game's AI-predicted difficulty has shown her only 1-star songs for years. Then one new song appears — and it's not 1 star.

- **The Armageddon Game** — A chess player intercepts a numbers station. The encoded messages are using a chessboard as cipher. After decoding, the moves correlate to real-world events — each one worse than the last. A countdown they can't stop.

- **Curse of the Grove** — An investigative journalist sneaks into Bohemian Grove. His intrusion interrupts the main ritual, starting a countdown to the end of the world.

- **Taken Parody (Budget Version)** — A scammer takes $300 from a guy. He pledges to get it back. Liam Neeson energy, $300 stakes.

- **Diving Chamber** — Three guys are trapped in a diving chamber after a catastrophic rapid decompression. Based on the Byford Dolphin incident. Claustrophobic thriller.

- **Post-Apocalypse Entertainer** — After the collapse of society, one man makes it his sole purpose to keep people entertained.

- **Wendigo House** — *When'd It Go?* — A couple moves into a house in the woods. Haunted by Wendigos. Horror/slow-burn.

- **How It's Made: Magic Items** — "How It's Made" but for magical items from fiction universes. D&D, Harry Potter, LOTR. Straight-faced industrial narration about wand crafting and ring forging.

- **Siblings and the Real Killer** — Two siblings try to prove their father's innocence. The real killer: their mother.

- **Venus Airship** — A man wakes up on an abandoned NASA Venus airship with no memory. He must make contact and escape before the ship sinks into the atmosphere.

- **Pet Sematary: The Toy** — A version of Pet Sematary where a beloved toy keeps coming back after being buried. Dad-focused. Gets darker with each return.

- **Slasher Film Crew** — Horror comedy: a film crew trying to make an 80s slasher movie gets killed off like an 80s slasher movie.

- **Bruce Almighty x Click (God Mode)** — Same premise as both films, but the remote/power is an iPhone and the interface is called God Mode.

- **Wrong Music on the Baby Monitor** — You put your kid to bed, turn on the loop music. Check the cam a few hours later. Different music is playing.

- **Goonies-Style Slumdog** — An Americanized, less dark, more whimsical Slumdog Millionaire. A small group of friends in an absurd situation, each using skills learned from video games and nerdy hobbies to survive and win.

- **Amongst Us** — Parody of Among Us. Fully committed bit.

- **Apple Watch From the Future** — A person slowly notices their Apple Watch is giving notifications about events that haven't happened yet.

## TV / Series Concepts

- **SCP Webseries** — A scientist is being interrogated by the military. Each episode is a different SCP encounter, building toward a containment breach.

- **SIN101** — (Play on CINE.) A college horror film studies course mysteriously connected to student deaths.

- **AI as Reviewer** — Can AI play D&D? Can AI play Pokémon? Experimental content series.

- **Ghost Recon: Wildlands Adaptation** — Joint op: CIA + DEA + JSOC taking down a cartel that has taken over Bolivia. Tom Clancy tone.

- **CIA Shadow Company Thriller** — Someone shorts stock in a CIA shadow company. Becomes a person of interest. Cat-and-mouse series.

- **Accepted But at a Mall** — *Accepted* energy but instead of a fake college it's a fake mall.

- **Last Dance** — A theater is haunted by the ghost of a dead dancer who takes out revenge on other dancers. Can be camp or genuinely creepy.

- **Neighboring Houses** — A story about 4–5 neighboring houses, told from the perspective of each house watching the others. The reveal: the narrator is itself a house — at a dead end.
