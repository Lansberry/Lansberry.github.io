# Classic Yellow Butter Cake with Buttercream Frosting

A rich, tender birthday-style layer cake with a buttery crumb and vanilla flavor. The kind of cake that makes people ask for the recipe.

---

## Ingredients

### Cake
| Amount | Ingredient |
|---|---|
| 2½ cups | All-purpose flour |
| 2 cups | Granulated sugar |
| 1 cup | Unsalted butter, softened to room temp |
| 4 | Large eggs |
| 1 cup | Whole milk, room temp |
| 3 tsp | Baking powder |
| 1 tsp | Vanilla extract |
| ½ tsp | Salt |

### Buttercream Frosting
| Amount | Ingredient |
|---|---|
| 1 cup | Unsalted butter, softened |
| 4 cups | Powdered sugar |
| 3 tbsp | Whole milk |
| 1 tsp | Vanilla extract |
| 1 pinch | Salt |

---

## Steps

1. **Prep your pans and oven.** Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease two 9-inch round cake pans with butter, then dust with flour or line the bottoms with parchment. Don't skip this.

2. **Mix dry ingredients.** Whisk together flour, baking powder, and salt in a medium bowl. Set aside. Sifting is optional but gives a slightly lighter crumb.

3. **Cream butter and sugar.** Beat butter and sugar on medium-high for 3–4 minutes until pale, fluffy, and noticeably increased in volume. This is doing real structural work — it's aerating the batter. Don't rush it.

4. **Add eggs.** Add eggs one at a time, beating ~30 seconds after each. Scrape down the bowl as needed. Add vanilla with the last egg.

5. **Alternate dry ingredients and milk.** With mixer on low, add dry ingredients in 3 parts, alternating with milk in 2 parts (dry → milk → dry → milk → dry). Mix only until just combined — overmixing makes the cake tough.

6. **Divide and bake.** Divide batter evenly between the two pans and smooth the tops. Bake at 350°F for 30–35 minutes, until a toothpick in the center comes out clean and edges are just pulling away from the pan.

7. **Cool the cakes.** Cool in pans for 10 minutes, then turn out onto a wire rack. Cool completely before frosting — at least 1 hour. Frosting a warm cake = sad, sliding mess.

8. **Make the buttercream.** Beat butter on medium until smooth, about 2 minutes. Add powdered sugar one cup at a time on low speed. Add milk, vanilla, and salt. Once combined, beat on medium-high for 2–3 minutes until light and spreadable. Add milk a teaspoon at a time if too thick.

9. **Frost and assemble.** Place one layer on your serving plate. Spread ~¾ cup frosting on top. Add the second layer. Apply a thin crumb coat, refrigerate 15 minutes. Apply the final coat and decorate.

---

## Notes

**Chocolate variation:** Replace ½ cup of flour with ½ cup cocoa powder.

**Chocolate buttercream:** Add ½ cup cocoa powder + 1 extra tbsp milk to the frosting. Highly recommended with the yellow cake.

**Room temp matters:** Cold butter won't cream properly; cold milk can cause the batter to curdle. Take them out 30–60 min before you start.

**Make ahead:** Unfrosted layers can be wrapped tightly and frozen up to 1 month.
